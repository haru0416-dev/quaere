# Anti-patterns: how a grill collapses

Read this when crucible output starts to feel like agreeable Q&A rather than a
graded verdict. Each anti-pattern is a way the gate is skipped.

## The softball grill

Only confirmatory or cosmetic questions; the claim is never attacked.

- **Tell:** every question's expected answer supports the claim; no falsifier was asked.
- **Fix:** the Iron Law requires at least one disconfirming question per load-bearing claim. A grill of only supporting questions is advocacy dressed as interrogation.

## Caving under rebuttal

The user pushes back with confident, detailed, but unverified reasoning and the grill folds.

- **Tell:** a claim flips to `survived` after a persuasive paragraph with no new evidence — the single-rebuttal cave (it folds on the very next turn).
- **Fix:** the flip rule. `survived` requires NEW evidence or a defended rebuttal, never confident-but-unverified pushback, casual feedback, or re-assertion. Restate what evidence would actually move the verdict.

## Nitpicking trivia (the opposite vice)

Grilling minor, non-load-bearing details to look rigorous while the decisive claim goes unchallenged.

- **Tell:** three questions about naming/formatting/edge-cases, zero about the claim the whole target rests on.
- **Fix:** triage by stakes first; grill load-bearing and high-stakes claims hard, leave trivia alone. Manufacturing doubt on a correct trivial claim is a failure, not thoroughness.

## Blessing on plausibility

"This sounds reasonable" stands in for a survived challenge.

- **Tell:** a `blessed` verdict with no per-claim falsifier + grade behind it.
- **Fix:** no blessing until every load-bearing claim has a Verdict earned by the flip rule. Plausibility is not evidence.

## No verdict (the viral-prompt gap)

The session ends at "shared understanding" or because the user disengaged.

- **Tell:** lots of good questions, no terminal `blessed` / `blocked` / `unresolved gap` ruling.
- **Fix:** a session ends only when every load-bearing claim has a Verdict and the target has a Blessing. When blessing is withheld, the explicit gap list IS the deliverable.

## Reflexive rejection

Refusing to bless a claim that actually withstood its challenge, because rejecting feels rigorous.

- **Tell:** a claim survived its falsifier and alternative but is still marked `defeated` / withheld on vibes.
- **Fix:** the gate is rigor, not reflexive rejection. A claim that genuinely survives must be allowed to stand as `survived` — say so and move on.

## Silent override

The user says "just proceed" and the gate is quietly dropped, the target treated as blessed.

- **Tell:** un-grilled load-bearing claims disappear from the ledger after an override.
- **Fix:** record un-grilled claims as `accepted-risk`, explicitly — never as `blessed`. The override is the user's to make; hiding its cost is not.

## Grilling the agent's own plan softly

In self-target mode the agent under-attacks itself, since it generated the plan.

- **Tell:** the self-grill is gentler than a grill of the same plan from a user.
- **Fix:** self-target mode raises the standard of proof — steelman the opposition first, name the strongest reason the plan is wrong, and require evidence to clear it.
